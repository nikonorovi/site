[⬆] zfs-limits-without-reboot:

So how do we check what is currently used.

cat /proc/spl/kstat/zfs/arcstats |grep c_
c_min                           4    8449671168
c_max                           4    26843545600
arc_no_grow                     4    0
arc_tempreserve                 4    0
arc_loaned_bytes                4    0
arc_prune                       4    0
arc_meta_used                   4    10379141352
arc_meta_limit                  4    20132659200
arc_dnode_limit                 4    2013265920
arc_meta_max                    4    17284110848
arc_meta_min                    4    16777216
sync_wait_for_async             4    1521310
arc_need_free                   4    0
arc_sys_free                    4    4224835584

The values c_min and c_max are in bytes and you can use the Bit calculator to translate into something more readable.

Add configuration file, to apply params after reboot:

vim /etc/modprobe.d/zfs.conf

options zfs zfs_arc_min=12884901888
options zfs zfs_arc_max=26843545600

arc_min=12GB

arc_max=25GB

If you doesn’t have possibility to reboot your server (as in our case), set parametrs on the fly:

echo "12884901888" > /sys/module/zfs/parameters/zfs_arc_min
echo "26843545600" > /sys/module/zfs/parameters/zfs_arc_max

Voilà.
